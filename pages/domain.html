Domain Information

Here you will find domain details about our project. The sections below describe the information gathered through our literature survey and the steps we've taken to address the research problem.

Literature Survey

The integration of virtual learning environments (VLEs) and personalized learning has shown significant potential in enhancing IT education. Research highlights the importance of adapting educational platforms to students' learning styles, improving both engagement and academic performance through models such as VARK and Kolb’s Experiential Learning Theory. Studies also emphasize the effectiveness of collaborative learning tools, like virtual study groups, which foster deeper understanding and peer-to-peer interaction in IT subjects that demand both theoretical knowledge and practical skills. Furthermore, the application of algorithm-driven academic recommendations offers a more refined approach, guiding students toward fields such as Software Engineering or Data Science based on their performance in critical modules, rather than relying solely on GPA

Research Gap

While current educational technologies have made strides in integrating virtual learning environments (VLEs) and personalized learning, significant gaps remain in tailoring these systems specifically to the diverse needs of IT students. Most platforms focus on general academic performance metrics, such as GPA, which fail to capture the nuanced strengths of students in key technical modules. Moreover, existing collaborative learning tools often overlook the importance of grouping students based on complementary learning styles, which could enhance peer-to-peer interaction and problem-solving. Additionally, the use of algorithm-driven recommendations for field specialization remains underexplored, with limited research on how these systems can be optimized to provide more personalized academic guidance. Addressing these gaps could significantly improve the effectiveness of educational platforms, offering more tailored and impactful learning experiences for IT students.

Research Problem

Current educational platforms in IT struggle to offer personalized guidance that truly reflects a student’s unique strengths in key technical subjects. By focusing primarily on GPA, these systems miss the deeper insights that could guide students toward fields like Software Engineering or Data Science. Additionally, virtual group study tools often ignore individual learning styles, limiting the potential for effective collaboration. The lack of an intelligent, algorithm-driven system that tailors academic recommendations based on specific module performance creates a gap, leaving students without the personalized support they need to make informed academic and career decisions.

Research Objectives

  • Develop a personalized virtual group study platform that tailors group formation based on students’ individual learning styles, enhancing collaboration and peer interaction.

  • Design an algorithm-driven field recommendation system that provides personalized academic guidance by analyzing students' performance in key IT modules, such as Object-Oriented Programming and Data Structures.

  • Evaluate the effectiveness of personalized learning approaches in improving student engagement, knowledge retention, and academic performance in IT education.

  • Bridge the gap between traditional GPA-based metrics and specific technical competencies, offering more precise recommendations for specialization in fields like Software Engineering or Data Science.

  • Create a user-friendly platform that integrates both the virtual study group and recommendation system, offering a holistic, student-centered educational experience.

Methodology

This research combines advanced technologies and educational strategies to build a personalized virtual platform that enhances both collaborative learning and academic guidance. A comprehensive, three-dimensional learning styles assessment is used to gather data on students' sensory, cognitive, and technical preferences. Using machine learning algorithms, students are dynamically grouped into virtual study teams, optimizing collaboration by aligning complementary learning styles.
For academic guidance, a decision-tree algorithm analyzes students' performance in critical modules to recommend specialized fields such as Software Engineering or Data Science. The algorithm evaluates module-specific grades, providing personalized feedback and suggestions for improvement. The platform was validated using historical academic data to ensure its accuracy in predicting successful field outcomes. Through this dual approach, the system fosters both immediate academic support and long-term career alignment.
